Why Copilot & Claude Code stay blocked

Your best people can't touch the best AI tools — for good reasons.

01

Code leaves the EU

US-hosted assistants send your source, research data and prompts to American servers. Under the US CLOUD Act and post-Schrems II scrutiny, that's a line legal and DPO offices won't cross.

02

No contract they can sign

Public institutions can't approve an opaque per-seat US SaaS. No DPA on acceptable terms, no transparency — and procurement simply says no.

03

Student & research data at risk

Anything touching personal or sensitive research data falls under GDPR. A black-box model in another jurisdiction is a non-starter for ethics boards.

04

It can be switched off

A tool your institution depends on can be revoked by a foreign government overnight. Dependency on it is now a strategic vulnerability, not a convenience.

How it works

Three open layers. One sovereign stack.

MakeMode isn't a new model — it's a thin, transparent layer that assembles proven open components into something an institution can own, inspect and host.

  • Swap the backend in one line — move from a hosted EU endpoint to your own GPUs without changing the workflow.
  • Inspect everything — open source end to end; your security team can read the code that handles your code.
  • Stay current — a thin layer, not a hard fork; you ride upstream improvements instead of maintaining a clone.
