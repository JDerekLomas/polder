Why higher ground

For two thousand years, before the dikes, the people of the low countries raised mounds of earth by hand to keep their homes above the floodwater. They didn't wait to be rescued. They built the ground they needed.

Europe's institutions face a rising tide of dependence on AI they don't control — models that can be priced, throttled, or switched off by decisions made an ocean away. MakeMode is the high ground they build for themselves: sovereign, open, and standing on European soil.

The rising tide

The dependency was invisible — until the water rose.

In June 2026, Washington ordered an American AI company to cut its models off from every foreign national overnight. The lesson landed across every European campus and boardroom at once: capability you rent can be taken back.

01

It leaves your jurisdiction

US-hosted assistants send your source, research and prompts across the Atlantic. Under the CLOUD Act, that's a line European legal and data-protection offices will not cross.

02

It can be revoked

A tool your people depend on can be throttled or shut off by a foreign government, or a vendor's change of terms, with no recourse. Dependence is now a strategic risk.

03

It can't be signed off

No DPA on acceptable terms, no transparency, no auditability. Procurement and ethics boards have nothing to approve — so the best tools stay banned.

04

It puts data at risk

Student records and sensitive research fall under GDPR. A black-box model in another country is a non-starter the moment real data is involved.

It runs where you say it runs

Your prompts never leave the continent.

A sovereign EU cloud, a national data centre, or your own racks. The weights are yours; the jurisdiction is European; the audit trail is one you can read.
